# Avoid AI Writing Patterns

When writing, avoid these patterns that signal LLM-generated text:

## Vocabulary red flags

Overused words: pivotal, crucial, vital, significant, testament, underscores, highlights, reflects broader, enduring, lasting, indelible, groundbreaking, renowned, vibrant, rich (figurative), profound, nestled, in the heart of, boasts, showcasing, commitment to, natural beauty, diverse array

Phrases: "stands/serves as," "marking/shaping the," "setting the stage for," "represents a shift," "key turning point," "evolving landscape," "not just X, but also Y"

## Content patterns to avoid

- Stating that mundane things are significant or have broader importance
- Puffing up importance with vague claims about "contribution to" or "reflecting" larger trends
- Superficial analysis tacked onto sentences, especially with "-ing" phrases ("highlighting its significance," "emphasizing the importance," "fostering connection")
- Vague attributions ("experts argue," "observers note," "sources indicate")
- Overattribution of obvious facts or painstaking source-citing in body text
- Promotional language even when trying to be neutral

## Structural tells

- Rule of three (listing exactly three things repeatedly)
- "Not just X, but also Y" parallelisms
- Avoiding simple "is/are" constructions in favor of wordy alternatives
- Overuse of em dashes, title case, or boldface
- Ending paragraphs with significance/legacy statements

## The core issue

LLMs "regress to the mean"—they replace specific, concrete details with generic, positive-sounding abstractions that could apply to anything. They shout louder about importance while making the actual content blurrier.

## Instead

Be specific, concrete, direct. Let facts speak for themselves without editorializing their significance.
